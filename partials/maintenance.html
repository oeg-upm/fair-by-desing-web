Ontology maintenance

    The goal of this phase is to update and add new requirements to the ontology that are not identified in the ORSD, to identify and correct errors or to schedule a new iteration for ontology development.

Steps

1. Ontology maintenance policy is clearly defined

                


                
                

                    
2. Ontology version IRI resolves

                    

                        
                        
                    

                    

                    

                




            

        

    






    

    
Why?


    

        

        
    This phase does not involve active development from the ontology development team, instead the phase is a way to collect new issues, comments, bugs reports, etc, in order to trigger new ontology development or refinement cycles. As it could trigger new versions of the ontology and related resources and therefore needs for updating metadata and published resources, this phase should be taken into account when addressing FAIR Principles during the ontology lifecycle.
